Language Support The Easy Way

If you do all the other steps properly, but you do not have the proper locales support in your Linux system, gettext() still won't work.

Most modern Linux distros allow you to click install a locale. For example, in Ubuntu Lucid, you merely go to Start->System->Administration->Language Support to install and configure the proper language.

To find out what support you all ready have installed you may do: 

locale -a

To test this change, you may do: 

export LANG="es_US.utf8"

Now every program, that has US Spanish support, will talk to you in Spanish when invoked from that console.

If you have accomplished this, you are done and may skip the rest of this chapter, otherwise, read on.
